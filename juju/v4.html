📚 Key Terms

Proportion — Two ratios (fractions) that are equal to each other. Example: 2/3 = 4/6

Cross Multiplication — To solve a proportion, multiply diagonally. If a/b = c/d, then a×d = b×c.

Ratio — A comparison of two quantities. Can be written as a:b or a/b.

Percent — A part out of 100. The word "per cent" literally means "per 100".

Percent Proportion — (part / whole) = (percent / 100). Use this to set up percent problems.

Unit Rate — A rate per ONE unit. Examples: miles per hour, dollars per pound, pages per minute.

Similar Figures — Shapes that have the same proportions. Their corresponding sides form equal ratios.

Better Buy — The option with the lower unit rate (less cost per item or per pound).

🔢 Section 1: Solving Basic Proportions

A proportion is two ratios set equal to each other. To solve, you cross multiply: multiply diagonally and set the products equal.

Example: Solve x8 = 34
Cross multiply: 4x = 8 × 3 = 24. So x = 24 ÷ 4 = 6.

Solve for x: x35 = 25

💡 Cross multiply: 5 × x = 35 × 2

Solve for m: 6m = -1517.5

💡 Cross multiply: 6 × 17.5 = -15 × m

Solve for x: x-210 = 23
Round to the nearest tenth.

💡 Cross multiply: 3(x-2) = 10 × 2 = 20. Then solve for x.

Solve for x: 56 = 7x+99
Round to the nearest tenth.

💡 Cross multiply: 5 × 9 = 6 × (7x+9). Then distribute and solve.

Solve for x: x-6x-7 = 92
Round to the nearest tenth.

💡 Cross multiply: 2(x-6) = 9(x-7). Distribute both sides.

Solve for x: 5x-9 = 8x+5
Round to the nearest tenth.

💡 Cross multiply: 5(x+5) = 8(x-9). Distribute both sides.

🔢 Section 2: Percent Problems (Set Up Proportions)

To solve any percent problem, set up the percent proportion:

partwhole = percent100

Example: What is 20% of 50?
x50 = 20100 → 100x = 1000 → x = 10

25% of what number is 28?

💡 Set up: 25/100 = 28/x. Cross multiply and solve.

What percent of 72 is 18?
Type just the number (no percent sign).

💡 Set up: x/100 = 18/72. Cross multiply.

60% of what number is 45?

💡 Set up: 60/100 = 45/x. Cross multiply.

What is 60% of 12?

💡 Set up: x/12 = 60/100. Cross multiply.

Mallory is knitting a blanket. She wants the blanket to be 10 feet long. The blanket is currently 5.75 feet long.
What percent of the blanket is done?
Type just the number.

💡 5.75 is the part. 10 is the whole. Set up the proportion.

Anthony spends 28% of his monthly income on rent. If his monthly income is $3,200, how much is his rent?

💡 x is part, 3200 is whole. Set up: 28/100 = x/3200.

🔢 Section 3: Word Problems with Proportions

To solve a word problem with a proportion, first set up a KEY showing what each part of the ratio means. Then write your proportion and cross multiply.

Example: A recipe uses 2 cups of flour for every 3 cookies. How much flour for 12 cookies?
Key: flour/cookies. Set up: 2/3 = x/12. Cross multiply: 3x = 24. x = 8 cups.

The park ranger stocks the children’s fishing pond keeping a ratio of 4 sunfish to 3 perch. If he puts 300 sunfish into the pond, how many perch should be put into the pond?

💡 Set up: sunfish/perch = 4/3 = 300/x. Cross multiply.

Sandra reads 5 pages every 4 minutes. How many pages can she read in 1 hour?
(Hint: 1 hour = 60 minutes)

💡 Set up: pages/minutes = 5/4 = x/60. Cross multiply.

Two triangles are similar (their sides are proportional). The small triangle has sides x and 12. The big triangle has the matching sides 56 and 84. Find x.

💡 Set up: small/small = big/big. So x/12 = 56/84.

A certain shade of green paint is made from 5 parts yellow mixed with 3 parts blue. If 2 cans of yellow are used, how many cans of blue should be used?

💡 Set up: yellow/blue = 5/3 = 2/x. Cross multiply.

🔢 Section 4: Unit Rates

A unit rate tells you how much for ONE thing.
To find a unit rate, divide the total amount by the number of units.

Example: If 4 apples cost $6.00, the unit rate is $6.00 ÷ 4 = $1.50 per apple.

A tire company charged the truck driver $5,400 to replace the 18 tires on his truck. Find the unit rate (cost per tire).

💡 Divide total cost by number of tires: 5400 / 18

It took 5 minutes for Hank to fill up his 40 gallon gas tank at the pump. Find the unit rate (gallons per minute).

💡 Divide gallons by minutes: 40 / 5

A flight from Philadelphia to Las Vegas (2,177 miles) takes 5 hours and 45 minutes. Find the unit rate (miles per hour). Round to the nearest tenth.
(Hint: 5 hours 45 minutes = 5.75 hours)

💡 Divide miles by hours: 2177 / 5.75

🔢 Section 5: Better Buy Comparisons

To find the better buy, calculate the unit rate (cost per unit) for each option. Whichever has the LOWER cost per unit is the better deal.

Example: A 10oz coffee for $5 vs a 16oz coffee for $7.
10oz: $5/10 = $0.50 per oz. 16oz: $7/16 ≈ $0.44 per oz. The 16oz is the better buy.

Carla can buy a 32 pound bag of dog food for $62.95 OR a 70 pound bag for $126.95.
Which is the better buy? (Type 32 or 70)

💡 Find the cost per pound for each. Whichever is cheaper per pound is the better buy.
